Why a cost in time calculator changes spending behavior

Money is easy to ignore because the number is abstract. Work time is harder to dismiss because it maps directly to effort, attention, and hours from your week.

Impulse check

A purchase can feel small in dollars but large in work hours. That pause is the point.

Better trade-offs

Timecostify helps compare purchases against the time you spend earning the money, which often leads to more deliberate choices.

Reusable habit

With widgets and saved decisions, the app turns one-off calculations into an ongoing spending discipline.

Frequently asked questions

These cover the main search intent around price to time calculators and salary-based purchase decisions.

How do you calculate the time cost of a purchase?

Divide the purchase price by your effective hourly rate. If you earn $30 per hour, a $120 purchase costs 4 hours of work.

What if I earn a monthly or yearly salary?

Convert your salary into an hourly rate using the number of hours you typically work each week, then use that rate for the calculation.

Is Timecostify only for strict budgeting?

No. It is also useful for people who want a calmer and more conscious relationship with spending, even if they do not follow a detailed budget.

Does the app support multiple currencies?

Yes. Timecostify includes multi-currency support so purchases remain understandable when shopping online or abroad.
